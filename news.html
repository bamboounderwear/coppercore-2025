Coppercore Inc. Announces $25 Million Equity Financing to Advance Mexico Copperbelt Development

McAllen, Texas / Monterrey, Mexico — July 3rd, 2025

Coppercore Inc., a U.S.-based copper exploration and development company operating in Coahuila, Mexico, has secured a $25 million equity financing agreement with Gulf Sea FZE, a UAE-based commodities investment firm.

Financing Structure

  • Initial $15 million closing expected on or before August 15th, 2025
  • Second tranche of $10 million contingent on achieving defined development milestones

Use of Proceeds

Proceeds will be used to advance exploration and early-stage production across Coppercore’s 2,000-hectare land package in Mexico’s Copperbelt, which hosts stratabound copper-silver mineralization comparable to major global deposits. The company plans to initiate a 10,000-meter drilling program and complete commissioning of its Phase One production facility by the first quarter of 2026.

  • Phase One production targeted at up to 3,000 metric tons per month of high-grade copper concentrate
  • Phase Two expansion anticipated to increase capacity to 6,000 metric tons per month by the fourth quarter of 2026

“Copper is essential to electrification and the energy transition. This financing positions us to accelerate development at a time of structurally tightening global supply.”

Antonio Treminio, CEO of Coppercore Inc.

According to industry estimates, global copper consumption reached approximately 26 million metric tons in 2024, with projections indicating potential supply deficits of 3.5 million to 10 million metric tons by 2030.

“Our investment in Coppercore is a strategic commitment to securing long-term exposure to critical copper assets vital for global electrification.”

Spokesperson, Gulf Sea FZE

The company is also evaluating additional options to support development, including a potential public listing and the issuance of copper-backed digital instruments.
